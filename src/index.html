Information requests jumped after NDP gained power in 2017

  • This chart shows every freedom of information (FOI) request submitted to the provincial government from 2010 to Oct. 4, 2021. ‘General’ requests are those seeking information on government operations, data or other non-personal information.

    Scroll for more...

  • We will remove ‘personal’ FOI requests from the analysis, since the Premier has said the current bill will not consider fees for personal information, only requests for government info.

  • Here we can see what kind of groups are filing FOI requests. Political parties have always made up a significant portion of requests. That jumped from 2018-2020, and is a key reason cited by the NDP for the need to limit FOI requests.

  • The amendments could have a chilling effect on the many non-political groups that routinely request information from the government, including individuals, media and other groups such as law firms, businesses, researchers and others.

    Requests from non-political parties have not increased alongside those form political parties, leading some critics to suggest that the public will be the ones paying the price over a fight between political parties.
